Second order derivative

Second order derivative determines the quadratic term in function Local approximation, it is the measurement of curvature (convexity)

  • f′′=0f'' = 0f′′=0 : Flat
  • f′′>0f'' > 0f′′>0 : Convex
  • f′′<0f'' < 0f′′<0 : Concave
  • ∥f′′∥\|f''\|∥f′′∥ : Determine curvature

Directional derivative view (bilinear form)

Consider f:Rn→Rf: \mathbb{R}^{n} \rarr \mathbb{R}f:Rn→R, x∈Rn\pmb{x} \in \mathbb{R}^{n}xx∈Rn, f(x)∈Rf(\pmb{x}) \in \mathbb{R}f(xx)∈R, H∈Rn×n\bm{H} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}H∈Rn×n

H\bm{H}H is symmetric, a.k.a. Hij=Hji\bm{H}_{ij} = \bm{H}_{ji}Hij​=Hji​, wherever the second order partial derivatives are continuous. Thus, it can be decomposed into orthogonal eigen basis.

H=QΛQTΛ=QTHQ\bm{H} = \bm{Q} \bm{\Lambda} \bm{Q}^T \\ \bm{\Lambda} = \bm{Q}^T \bm{H} \bm{Q}H=QΛQTΛ=QTHQ

More see Eigendecomposition and Eigenbasis

The second order Directional Derivative of direction vector d\bm{d}d is given by

dTHd\bm{d}^T \bm{H} \bm{d}dTHd

If d\bm{d}d is eigenvector, the result is its corresponding eigenvalue.
