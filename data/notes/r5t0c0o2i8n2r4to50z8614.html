Partial Derivative (Jacobian Matrix)

Let f:Rn→Rf: \mathbb{R}^{n} \rarr \mathbb{R}f:Rn→R, x∈Rn\pmb{x} \in \mathbb{R}^{n}xx∈Rn, f(x)∈Rf(\pmb{x}) \in \mathbb{R}f(xx)∈R:

J=∇xf=[∂f(x)∂x1∂f(x)∂x2…∂f(x)∂xn]∈R1×n\bm{J} = \nabla_{\pmb{x}}f = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial f(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times n}J=∇xx​f=[∂x1​∂f(xx)​​∂x2​∂f(xx)​​…​∂xn​∂f(xx)​​]∈R1×n

Let f:Rn→Rmf: \mathbb{R}^{n} \rarr \mathbb{R}^{m}f:Rn→Rm, x∈Rn\pmb{x} \in \mathbb{R}^{n}xx∈Rn, f(x)∈Rm\pmb{f}(\pmb{x}) \in \mathbb{R}^{m}ff(xx)∈Rm:

J=∇xf=[∂f1(x)∂x1∂f1(x)∂x2…∂f1(x)∂xn∂f2(x)∂x1∂f2(x)∂x2…∂f2(x)∂xn⋮⋮∂fm(x)∂x1∂fm(x)∂x2…∂fm(x)∂xn]∈Rm×n\bm{J} = \nabla_{\pmb{x}}\pmb{f} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_1(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial f_1(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial f_2(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_2(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial f_2(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & & & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_m(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_m(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial f_m(\pmb{x})}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}J=∇xx​ff=⎣⎡​∂x1​∂f1​(xx)​∂x1​∂f2​(xx)​⋮∂x1​∂fm​(xx)​​∂x2​∂f1​(xx)​∂x2​∂f2​(xx)​∂x2​∂fm​(xx)​​………​∂xn​∂f1​(xx)​∂xn​∂f2​(xx)​⋮∂xn​∂fm​(xx)​​⎦⎤​∈Rm×n

J\bm{J}J is known as the Jacobian Matrix
