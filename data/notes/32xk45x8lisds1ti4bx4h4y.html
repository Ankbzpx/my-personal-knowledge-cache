Mesh Deformation

Reference: https://lgg.epfl.ch/publications/2006/botsch_2006_DTD.pdf

Variational minimization approach

Suface S\mathcal{S}S = low-frequency based surface B\mathcal{B}B + high-frequency geometry details D\mathcal{D}D

Typical steps

  1. Compute B\mathcal{B}B from S\mathcal{S}S
  2. Encode details D\mathcal{D}D as displacement w.r.t. B\mathcal{B}B
  3. Deform B→B′\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{B}'B→B′
  4. Apply the details S′=B′+D\mathcal{S}' =\mathcal{B}' + \mathcal{D}S′=B′+D

Limitations

  1. Difference between S\mathcal{S}S and B\mathcal{B}B needs to be small enough that the a height field w.r.t. B\mathcal{B}B is sufficient to represent it (otherwise more hierarchies are needed)
  2. Displacement may cause self-intersection

Deformation Transfer

Definition

For a source mesh in original state S\mathcal{S}S (with vertex qi\bm{q}_iqi​) and deformed state S′\mathcal{S}'S′ (with vertex qi′\bm{q}_i'qi′​), a source deformation is computed (with gradient Sj\bm{S}_jSj​) for each triangle.

The per-triangle deformation Sj\bm{S}_jSj​ can be calculated as

Sj=(q1′−q3′,q2′−q3′,n′)⋅(q1−q3,q2−q3,n)−1\bm{S}_j = (\bm{q}_1' - \bm{q}_3', \bm{q}_2' - \bm{q}_3', \bm{n}') \cdot (\bm{q}_1 - \bm{q}_3, \bm{q}_2 - \bm{q}_3, \bm{n})^{-1}Sj​=(q1′​−q3′​,q2′​−q3′​,n′)⋅(q1​−q3​,q2​−q3​,n)−1

where qi\bm{q}_iqi​ qi′\bm{q}_i'qi′​ and n\bm{n}n n′\bm{n}'n′ are triangle vertex position and normal in S\mathcal{S}S or S′\mathcal{S}'S′.

The goal is to find new deformed vertex position for a target mesh, such that the target deformation gradient matches the source ones Sj\bm{S}_jSj​.

The new vertex position pi′\bm{p}_i'pi′​ can be calculated as

GTDG[p1′T⋮pn′T]=GTD[S1′T⋮Sm′T]\bm{G}^T \bm{D} \bm{G} \begin{bmatrix} {\bm{p}_1'}^T \\ \vdots \\ {\bm{p}_n'}^T \end{bmatrix} = \bm{G}^T \bm{D} \begin{bmatrix} {\bm{S}_1'}^T \\ \vdots \\ {\bm{S}_m'}^T \end{bmatrix}GTDG⎣⎡​p1′​T⋮pn′​T​⎦⎤​=GTD⎣⎡​S1′​T⋮Sm′​T​⎦⎤​

where G∈R3m×n\bm{G} \in \mathbb{R}^{3m \times n}G∈R3m×n as gradient operator (More see grad), D∈R3m×3m\bm{D} \in \mathbb{R}^{3m \times 3m}D∈R3m×3m is diagonal triangle-based weight matrix, GTD\bm{G}^T \bm{D}GTD is divergence operator, GTDG\bm{G}^T \bm{D} \bm{G}GTDG is divergence of gradient, a.k.a. discrete laplacian operator.

IMPORTANT Above equation only constraints gradient. Translational contraints are also needed when performing mesh deformation

Application in mesh deformation

  1. Compute B\mathcal{B}B from S\mathcal{S}S
  2. Deform B→B′\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{B}'B→B′ and compute per-triangle deformation gradient Sj\bm{S}_jSj​
  3. Transfer S→S′\mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S}'S→S′ by complying Sj\bm{S}_jSj​
