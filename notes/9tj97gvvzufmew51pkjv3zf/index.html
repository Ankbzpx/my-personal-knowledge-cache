Fitting

Homography Matrix H∈R3×3\bm{H} \in \mathbb{R}^{3 \times 3}H∈R3×3 has 8 DoF (H33=1H_{33} = 1H33​=1) that can be fit with 4 pairs of coplanar points.

Robust fitting

using Least Square (LS) with RANSAC, or Iteratively Reweighted Least Square

Decomposition

Homography Matrix H=R−tnTd\bm{H} = \bm{R} - \bm{t} \frac{\bm{n}^T}{d}H=R−tdnT​ can be decomposed into rotation R\bm{R}R, normalized translation td\frac{\bm{t}}{d}dt​ and plane normal n\bm{n}n

Solution ambiguity

Homography decomposition usually has 8 solutions

Non crossing contraints

4 of 8 can be filtered by checking if two cameraa are on the opposite of plane π\bm{\pi}π

tdTn<d′\frac{\bm{t}}{d}^T \bm{n} < d'dt​Tn<d′

Point visibility constraint

2 of remaining 4 can be filtered by checking if unprojected image points are in front of the plane

x^Tn>0x^′T(Rn)>0\bm{\hat{x}}^T \bm{n} > 0 \\ \bm{\hat{x}'}^T (\bm{R} \bm{n}) > 0x^Tn>0x^′T(Rn)>0
